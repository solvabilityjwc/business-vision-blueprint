BUSINESS VISION BLUEPRINT

Starting with the End in Mind™

This exercise is designed to help you start with the end in mind. By envisioning the business you truly want (its structure, purpose, culture, and impact) you will be able to reverse-engineer your current operations to align with that vision. Set aside an hour or more in a place that inspires you. Answer honestly. There are no right answers.

VISION AND PURPOSE
1. What inspired you to start this business, and how does it align with your personal values and passions?
  • What was the moment or reason that sparked your entrepreneurial journey?
  • How does your business express who you are at your core?
  • What values or causes are central to your business mission?
2. If your business was operating at its absolute best, what would it look like in 5 to 10 years?
  • What kind of reputation, clients, and growth would you have?
  • What core products or services would drive your business at that level?
  • How would you feel day-to-day in this version of your business?
3. What impact do you want your business to have on your community, industry, or the world?
  • What lasting legacy do you hope your business will leave?
  • What problem are you passionate about solving?
  • How do you want your business to influence your employees, clients, and stakeholders?
IDEAL BUSINESS MODEL
1. What type of clients or customers do you enjoy working with the most?
  • What characteristics make certain clients more fulfilling to work with?
  • How can you attract more of these ideal clients?
2. Are there products or services you no longer feel passionate about offering? What would you focus on instead?
  • What offerings feel outdated, cumbersome, or no longer exciting?
  • What new or untapped opportunities excite you right now?
3. What aspects of your business energize you, and which areas drain you?
  • How much time do you spend on activities you dislike versus those you enjoy?
  • What would delegating or automating the draining tasks look like?
WORK-LIFE INTEGRATION
1. How much time would you ideally spend working each week? What would a perfect workweek look like?
  • What specific activities would fill your perfect workweek?
  • What adjustments would you need to make to achieve that balance?
2. What personal goals would you like your business to support or enable?
  • How do you want your business to empower you to pursue personal goals?
  • How can your business model provide flexibility for personal fulfillment?
3. How can your business model allow for more freedom, flexibility, and fulfillment in your personal life?
  • Is your business structured to run effectively without you always being present?
  • What would you need to change to achieve more freedom in both business and life?
FINANCIAL SUCCESS AND SUSTAINABILITY
1. What revenue goals would allow you to feel both successful and satisfied?
  • Beyond survival, what revenue would truly fulfill your business aspirations?
  • How can you balance financial success with personal fulfillment?
  • Have you evaluated your relationship with money?
2. How can you structure your pricing or business model to generate the profit you need while reducing stress?
  • Is your current pricing reflective of the value you provide?
  • How can you simplify your offerings to increase profitability and reduce complexity?
3. What changes would you make if profitability and growth were not the only metrics of success?
  • How would you adjust your business if satisfaction and joy were equally important as revenue?
  • How would this perspective change your overall business strategy?
PERSONAL FULFILLMENT
1. How will you know when you have achieved the ideal version of your business?
  • What key indicators of success will let you know you have built the business you love?
  • How will you measure whether your business brings you joy and fulfillment?
